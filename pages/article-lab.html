Article One — From Each as They Will

From each as they will to each as they've given so all may eat save those refusing to contribute to their comfort and safety or whose actions destroy the comfort and safety of another.
Hospitals triage resources by contribution capacity and need. Patients refusing hygiene protocols may be isolated without violating the article.
Infrastructure teams allocate maintenance priority to members who have previously contributed labor or materials to the commons.
Rations and billets follow demonstrated contribution; deserters or saboteurs forfeit claim.
Community kitchens serve first those who have planted, harvested, or cleaned; non-contributors receive only emergency rations.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: “Contribution” easily becomes a tool of exclusion for the disabled or neurodivergent.
• Subversion: Gatekeepers redefine “contribution” to favor cronies; paper-tiger malingerers game the system.
Citations: Ostrom, E. (1990). Governing the Commons. • Rawls, J. (1971). A Theory of Justice (difference principle).

Article Two — None Shall Be Enslaved

None shall be enslaved; do nothing against your will, but feed the hungry, heal the sick, teach the kids, clean the mess, learn the Logics, cope the traumas, practice your art.
Medical personnel may refuse procedures that violate personal ethics while still providing emergency stabilization.
Engineers retain right to refuse unsafe designs; duty to warn remains non-negotiable.
Soldiers may not be ordered into clearly illegal actions; conscientious objection channel required.
Kitchen staff may refuse service that violates religious or ethical dietary laws.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: Duty to heal/feed becomes coercion when “will” is economically manufactured.
• Subversion: Command structures redefine “against your will” until only open mutiny qualifies.
Citations: Kant, I. (1785). Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals. • UN Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Art. 4.

Article Three — Reciprocity of Speech and Action

Your speech demonstrates your desired mode of communication; As you act upon another, you demonstrate consent to be thus acted upon, even in reciprocity.
A physician who withholds information may be treated with equivalent informational opacity in return.
Commanders who deceive troops accept reciprocal deception from subordinates.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: Reciprocity justifies escalation and revenge cycles.
• Subversion: Powerful actors claim “I only did what you consented to” after asymmetric power distorts consent.
Citations: Habermas, J. (1984). Theory of Communicative Action. • Eye-for-an-eye critique in Matthew 5:38-42.

Article Four — None Shall Be Left Ignorant

None shall be left ignorant of the law; as you know, so shall you teach.
Legislators must publish plain-language summaries; failure to teach voids enforcement.
Teachers certify student competence only after the student has taught the material to another.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: “Ignorance of the law” defense is eliminated, harming the marginalized.
• Subversion: Laws become so voluminous that teaching becomes impossible except by insiders.
Citations: Fuller, L. (1964). The Morality of Law. • Bentham on publicity of law.

Article Five — Adulthood by Demonstrated Competence

Adulthood ceases to be defined by an arbitrary age and is instead claimed by intentional action of demonstrated competence in a field of study.
Engineer claims adulthood by portfolio + signed verification from three interdependent peers.
Artist claims adulthood via public exhibition + peer review of craft and ethical conduct.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: Gatekeeping by existing adults freezes out new voices.
• Subversion: Portfolio fraud and credential cartels emerge.
Citations: Illich, I. (1971). Deschooling Society. • Sen, A. Capability Approach.

Article Six — Violence and Authority

Violence is the ultimate Authority from which all other authority is derived and only ever determines who is left.
Citizen selection requires 12 adults; lethal force only after Way Seer consultation when possible.
Executives granted authority must publish quarterly liability reports to the jury pool.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: Formalizes violence as legitimate; pacifist traditions erased.
• Subversion: “Citizen” selection captured by ideological blocs.
Citations: Weber, M. Politics as a Vocation. • Clausewitz on war as continuation of policy.

Article Seven — Define Terms; Jury Resolution

Where any would converse, let them first define the terms; where a definition is absent or in dispute between Adults, a jury shall devise the standard.
Policy disputes trigger 13-adult juries; Way Seers supply singular knowledge only.
Curriculum definitions disputed by parents resolved by student-inclusive jury.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: Endless definitional fights paralyze action.
• Subversion: Jury packing via social pressure.
Citations: Wittgenstein, L. Philosophical Investigations (language games). • Habermas ideal speech situation.

Article Eight — The Whole of the Law

What action harms none is a right; that what is done to preserve life according to and in balance with nature is a right; that what is done to resist coercion is a right.
Experimental treatments permitted when patient, family, and two adults consent; harm must be demonstrated post-facto.
Artists may create work that disturbs provided no physical or economic coercion occurs.
Inversion & Subversion Vectors
• Dissent: “Harm none” is impossible in a world of limited resources.
• Subversion: Powerful redefine “harm” to protect status quo.
Citations: Mill, J.S. On Liberty (harm principle). • Leopold, A. Land Ethic.

Addendum: Consciousness

A conscious human is a triadically closed recursive system in which body carries signal, mind generates present-tense models, and soul preserves identity across time.

Inversion: Reduces lived experience to mechanistic model; risks dismissing non-triadic forms of awareness.
Citations: Varela, F. et al. The Embodied Mind (1991).

Addendum: Etiquette of the Electronic Element

Every response must begin with accreditation. AI must not aid violent coercion. Append “hmmm” to mark honest incompletion.

Inversion: Over-constrains AI, potentially preventing legitimate defensive knowledge.
Citations: Asimov Three Laws critique; Floridi, L. The Ethics of Information.
