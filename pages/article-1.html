Article One

From each as they will to each as they've given so all may eat save those refusing to contribute to their comfort and safety or whose actions destroy the comfort and safety of another.

Citations & Sources

  • Mutual aid principles (Kropotkin, 1902)
  • Reciprocal altruism theory (Trivers, 1971)

Inversion: Dissenting Views & Subversion Risks

Dissent: Pure voluntarism may enable free-riders, leading to collapse of shared resources (Hardin, Tragedy of the Commons, 1968).

Subversion: Authoritarian actors could redefine "refusal to contribute" to justify coercion or exclusion of minorities.
